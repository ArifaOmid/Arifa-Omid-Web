Chicken Food is Our Food

According to "Arsenic in Our Chicken?" chickens are fed banned antibiotics, caffeine, and possibly arsenic. Arsenic is known to cause arsenic-poisoning, yet they are fed to chickens to keep them healthy. Also, they may not kill all the harmful bacteria. The remaining bacteria that can survive will grow and create a superbug!


Consumer Reports

The superbug that these chickens create and carry will be passed on through their waste and when they are dead. Humans end up handling their waste and carcasses. Some will end up eating the chickens unknowingly! This means that humans will be infected by the superbug, which will not be cured by the regular antibiotics.

Antibiotic-resistant infections are not always severe, but they can slowly harm one's well-being, especially if it is unable to be cured. Arsenic poisoning, on the other hand, can cause severe symptoms and death according to the WHO organization, which should make people wary of what processing has been done.
